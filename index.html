About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Department of Computing Sciences at Bocconi University and a member of MilaNLP. I work at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Computational Social Science, and socially aware language technologies.

My research focuses on how hate speech is expressed and interpreted across languages, communities, and cultural contexts. I am especially interested in implicit hate speech, implicit misogyny and classism. I am also interested in the ways identity is encoded in datasets and language models, and how different identities interact with them.

More broadly, I am interested in building NLP systems that are sensitive to identity, context, and the people affected by the technologies we design.
